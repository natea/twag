## Description

Loop is an invite-only supper on the recursive patterns behind the physical economy — control loops, feedback loops, scaling laws.

It’s for founders and investors working in manufacturing, field services, construction, energy, logistics, defense, and humanoids — the companies where revenue is earned at an address, not a URL.

Most tech dinners happen in the world of bits — SaaS metrics, model benchmarks, ARR curves. Loop is the other table. It’s for the people building and backing the companies where revenue is earned at a job site, a factory floor, a warehouse, or an address on a map.

The conversation has one spine: the recursive patterns that govern physical revenue. Control loops. Feedback loops. Scaling laws. The physical world doesn’t compound like software — but it does compound. Understanding how is the whole game.

Twelve seats, one long table, a pop-up chef, and natural wine from growers around the globe. Five courses built on a single scaling rule. An Escher print on the menu, a Duchamp quote chosen for each guest, and a quiet personalization on each plate. No decks, no panels, no pitches.

Loop is hosted by Allometry — the agentic operating substrate for physical revenue. Modern industrial operators run every commercial decision through Allometry: every quote, every contract, every dispatch, every renewal, scored against margin and EV at the address level. Each decision is signed at write-time and accumulates in the operator’s vault — a hash-chained, time-locked ledger of operational truth. That vault becomes the interchange standard between operators and institutional capital: continuous quality of earnings, four lender tiers, one ledger underneath. The same control layer that prices a quote today underwrites working capital tomorrow, infrastructure debt next, and makes labor, machines, and humanoids programmable next after that.

If you want to scan the thesis ahead of the table, allometry.com/thesis is the five-minute version — six atoms, with a toggle for the anti-thesis on the reverse. The architecture lives one layer deeper at  allometry.com/the-vault.

The dinner isn’t about Allometry. It’s about the people at the table.

Greenpoint. $295/seat + service. Covers chef, wine pairing, and room. Waived for Allometry design partners. Every seat earns 1 Loop — a free Vault Audit on a real operation, redeemable whenever, or giftable to a portfolio company.


Twelve seats, four kinds of guests:

— Operators building revenue at the address — manufacturing, field services, construction, energy, logistics, defense, humanoids.

— Lenders and capital allocators underwriting the physical economy — banks, infrastructure debt, insurance-linked, pension, rated paper.

— Venture investors in industrial, infrastructure, and agentic AI.

— Partners — channel, implementation, and capital, building alongside.

If you sit at one of those tables, write to hello@allometry.com.
