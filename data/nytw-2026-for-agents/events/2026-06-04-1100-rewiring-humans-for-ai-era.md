## Description

Rewiring Humans for the AI Era

The Rise of the $26T Brain Economy: What Founders, Corporates, and Investors Need to Understand to Build the Next Unicorn

AI is scaling faster than human biology can adapt to it.

While companies race to deploy models, agents, and automation, a new invisible frontier is emerging: the neurobiological infrastructure behind human performance.

This session explores how:

* cognitive overload,
* attention fragmentation,
* chronic stress,
* motivation neurochemistry,
* and nervous system regulation

are becoming critical economic variables in the AI era.

In a world where productivity, creativity, decision-making, and adaptability define competitive advantage, the next generation of billion-dollar companies may not be built on software alone — but on human cognitive optimization.

A frontier conversation at the intersection of:

* neurobiology,
* AI readiness,
* brain capital,
* cognitive performance,
* attention economics,
* nervous system regulation,
* and the future of human adaptation.

Presented by Argentine neurotechnology company RobinTests, with the support of the Argentine Consulate in New York.

Join founder Noelia Aguirre for an exploration into why the AI economy may also become the Brain Economy — and what humans will need to become truly AI-ready.
