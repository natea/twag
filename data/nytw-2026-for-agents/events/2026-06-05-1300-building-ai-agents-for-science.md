## Description

Building AI Agents for Science: From Theory to Laboratory

Artificial intelligence is undergoing a paradigm shift, moving beyond simple chatbots to autonomous agents capable of complex reasoning. Nowhere is this transformation more critical than in the sciences, where AI agents are beginning to accelerate the pace of discovery, from drug development to material science.

Hosted by GRAIL, this session explores the "Autonomous Science" movement—a new frontier where PhD-level agents partner with researchers to automate the scientific loop. This two-part event features a high-level panel of academic and industry leaders followed by a hands-on technical workshop.

During the workshop, participants will be introduced to ApexClaw, GRAIL's flagship platform for building advanced science agents. Built on the open-source foundations of OpenClaw, ApexClaw provides the specialized reasoning and rigor required for scientific research.

What You’ll Learn
Participants will explore the architecture of scientific agents and the shift toward autonomous R&D, including:

#The Future of Research: A panel discussion with higher-education leaders and AI experts on how agents are reshaping the university lab.
#The Agentic Loop: Understanding how AI systems handle hypothesis generation, experimental planning, and data interpretation.
#Introduction to ApexClaw: How to leverage specialized frameworks to go beyond general-purpose LLMs.
#Live Implementation: A guided session on deploying your first autonomous agent for a scientific task.

Who Should Attend
This event is designed for:
#Founders and Researchers looking to integrate autonomous workflows into their R&D.
#PhD Students and Academics interested in the future of digital laboratory assistants.
#Engineers and Developers exploring the intersection of AI, OpenClaw, and specialized scientific domains.
#Investors interested in the "AI for Science" vertical.

What Participants Receive
All attendees will receive exclusive free credits for ApexClaw, allowing you to implement your own agent during the workshop and continue building your own digital researcher after the event concludes.
