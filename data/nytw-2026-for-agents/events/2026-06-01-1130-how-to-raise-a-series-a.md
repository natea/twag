## Description

Raising a Series A is a fundamentally different process than raising a seed round. Expectations shift—from vision to execution, from storytelling to metrics, and from promise to proof. This panel is designed for seed-stage founders who want a clear, realistic view of what it takes to successfully raise a Series A in today’s market.

You’ll hear directly from industry professionals as to what Series A VCs actually look for, how founders derail otherwise strong raises, and how to prepare months in advance—not weeks.

Target Audience:
- Seed-stage founders planning to raise a Series A in the next 6–18 months
- Founders who have product-market fit signals but want clarity on readiness
- CEOs navigating early traction, hiring, and go-to-market scale
Founders who want to avoid common Series A fundraising mistakes

Topics of Discussion:
- What “Series A ready” really means in 2026 (metrics, momentum, narrative)?
- What are the key diligence questions Series A investors focus on and why, including pre-raise legal and financial considerations?
- How Series A expectations differ from seed across product, GTM, and team?
-How does a founder run a tight Series A process (timing, data room, investor targeting)?
- Valuation, ownership, and control considerations founders often overlook when raising their first priced round.
