## Description

What if you could feel how giving shapes the nervous system in real time?

Totems of Hope is a neuroaesthetic immersive installation exploring presence, attention, and human connection through sound, spatial design, and non-invasive neurotechnology.

As participants move through the experience, physiological patterns subtly influence an evolving sensory environment — where sound, interaction, and neuroadaptive systems create a living feedback loop between inner state and space.

This is not a talk.  
It is something you step into.

As part of the experience, participants collectively prepare food resources that are redistributed to a local community, linking inner experience with tangible impact. The event gathers artists, technologists, founders, researchers, and cultural innovators around emerging conversations at the intersection of humanity and technology.

The experience will take place at Duca Studio by Duca Sartoria, bringing an additional layer of craftsmanship, elegance, and contemporary Italian design into the experiential environment — creating a dialogue between embodied technology, aesthetics, and human expression.

Presented internationally, including at AWE USA and LA Tech Week / Internet Tech Week @ FYI, and engaging over 1,000 participants internationally, the work sits at the intersection of immersive systems, human perception, and social impact.

A space to slow down, tune in, and experience technology differently — not as distraction, but as a medium for connection.

This event is part of NY Tech Week — a week of events hosted by VCs and startups bringing together the technology ecosystem.
