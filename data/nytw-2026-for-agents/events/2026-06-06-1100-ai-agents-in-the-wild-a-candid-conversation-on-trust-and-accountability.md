## Description

We’re deploying AI agents faster than we can verify them. 

As AI agents take on increasingly consequential roles, the question of trust has never been more urgent. What does it actually mean to trust AI agents and how do you build responsible infrastructure? 

We’re convening the best group of industry, product, and thought leaders alongside VCs, founders, and academics to explore the following topics: 

1. Defining trust and how companies/frontier labs currently evaluate trustworthiness
2. What is the gap between current governance frameworks and deployment?
3. How are high-stakes industries, like finance and healthcare, approaching AI agent deployment and what can others learn from them? 
4. What are early agent adopters learning in regulated settings? 

There is “responsible AI” as a principle. But this is a session on something more concrete: how to move from intuition-based trust to system-level guarantees - and what needs to exist, technically and institutionally, for that shift to happen.

Join us for an exciting chat with Bryant McCombs (OpenAI) and Diane Chang (Google) as well as roundtable discussions and networking to follow.
