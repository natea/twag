## Description

Future of Flight: Aerospace & Dual-Use Investor Forum

Join us during NY Tech Week for an RSVP-only investor forum focused on the future of aerospace, defense technology, aviation modernization, autonomy, UAS, advanced manufacturing, and dual use systems.

Aerospace is entering a new investment cycle. National security priorities, commercial aviation modernization, pilot and workforce constraints, industrial base limitations, autonomous systems, certification complexity, and supply chain resilience are converging to create a new class of hard tech opportunities.

This forum is designed for investors, founders, operators, and technical leaders who want to better understand how aerospace and dual use companies are built, financed, certified, protected, and scaled.

The evening will feature a moderated expert panel with perspectives across aircraft certification, aviation operations, venture capital, intellectual property, and aerospace company building.

Featured speakers include:

Donald Maloy
President & CEO, Maloy Aircraft
Founder of an emerging aircraft OEM developing the MA-1 Blue Jay, a clean-sheet training aircraft and integrated training system with relevance across civilian airline pilot training and military primary flight training.

Mike Borfitz
CEO & Partner, Kilroy Aviation
Aviation certification expert with decades of experience across FAA certification, delegation, production certification, and continued operational safety.

Dagmar Andersch
Co-Founder & CEO, Acelorix
International aviation safety, compliance, certification, and UAS operations expert with deep experience across commercial aviation, safety oversight, and operational risk.

J.P. Keating
Managing Partner & Founder, proChain Ventures
Early-stage investor focused on supply chain, logistics, mobility, autonomy, and critical technologies tied to economic resilience and national security.

Jose J. Jimenez
Partner, Polsinelli
Patent attorney and former Boeing aerospace engineer focused on patent protection for aerospace, AI, machine learning, UAV, rocket, and satellite technologies.

The discussion will translate complex aerospace and regulatory topics into practical investor judgment:

• How to evaluate aerospace and dual use companies
• What separates credible execution from technical hype
• Why certification and operating authority matter
• How customer adoption works in aerospace markets
• Where investors are seeing opportunity across autonomy, logistics, defense technology, and aviation modernization
• How intellectual property and technical defensibility affect company value

This is not a general networking mixer. It is a focused conversation for investors and leaders looking to understand the companies, technologies, and market forces shaping the next generation of flight.

Format
Networking and arrivals

Post-panel networking
