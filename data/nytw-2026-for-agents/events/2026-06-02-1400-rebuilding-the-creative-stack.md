## Description

The AI-first creative workflow is no longer theoretical; it’s happening now. Every week, a new tool promises to upend how work is produced, forcing designers, strategists, editors, and marketers to adapt or risk being pushed out.

This panel brings together creative leaders and AI builders to address what should change now, what the next five years will bring, and how to position yourself to lead the next wave of creative production.

About the Panel
We’re excited to be joined by the following panelists:

Savannah Wolf
DESIGN, PRIMARY VENTURES
Savannah leads design at Primary Ventures, an early-stage venture capital firm, partnering with founders to build brand foundations that support critical moments—from fundraising to securing early customers—as they define what they’re building and who they’re building it for. She has spent over a decade building products and design teams at early-stage startups across a wide range of industries, guided by a central question: how do complex human problems, good design, and early-stage chaos not only coexist but make each other better? She is also deeply curious about how AI will shape the products and experiences we design next, with a critical eye on what we should have learned by now and how creative problem-solving will shape a future worth being part of.

Louis Kishfy
FOUNDER, TRIE
Louis is the founder of Trie (trie.dev), an AI associate for the last mile of deliverables. Trie helps professional service businesses turn meetings and context into structured, production-ready output. Louis believes the best tools solve specific problems without replacing human judgment—reducing the friction between thinking clearly and making something real, while preserving the human texture that makes work meaningful.


Brian White
COFOUNDER, HOWDY

Brian is the cofounder of Howdy, a brand and marketing studio supporting both early stage startups and established household names like the Obama Foundation, Sanrio, and Wonder.

When it comes to AI, Brian is equal parts optimist and cynic. He sees its potential for freeing the workforce from tedious repetitive tasks while staying critical of the bar for quality across solutions.

Howdy created an AI-supported brand and marketing sprint, Leapday.ai. It combines senior-level expertise with AI speeds to meet the needs of startup speeds and budgets.
