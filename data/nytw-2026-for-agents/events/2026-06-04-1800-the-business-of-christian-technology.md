## Description

A night of conversation, founder pitches, networking, and thoughtful discussion exploring the intersection of faith, technology, AI, startups, and human flourishing in New York City.What does it look like to build technology that genuinely serves people well?

Faith-driven technology is no longer sitting on the sidelines. From prayer apps and AI tools to church infrastructure, Bible engagement products, donor platforms, and community-building software, Christian technology is becoming a real and growing part of the broader tech ecosystem.

Companies like Hallow and Gloo are demonstrating both meaningful demand and real opportunity in the space, while advances in AI are opening new possibilities for discipleship, ministry workflows, education, translation, and community-building.

But this night isn’t just about markets, trends, or business models.

It’s about asking deeper questions around ethics, formation, creativity, stewardship, and what Christians should build differently in a rapidly changing technological world.

The evening will include:

🎤 A live panel with founders, investors, and ministry leaders

⚡ Lightning pitches from builders creating in the faith-tech space

🤝 Networking with founders, engineers, creatives, operators, investors, and others from across NYC tech

🍸 Drinks, conversation, and community

Community + Collaboration

Christians*inTech exists to create welcoming spaces where believers and seekers can connect around faith, vocation, creativity, and technology in New York City.

Our hope for this gathering is simple:

To bring together thoughtful people building in technology and create space for conversations around faith, innovation, ambition, and human flourishing.
