## Description

New model drops. New framework. New "this changes everything" thread. By the time you've integrated one tool, three more have made it obsolete.

So we asked ourselves a different question: what doesn't change? Across enterprise teams, across codebases, across the hype cycles. Which engineering practices actually hold up when the underlying models rotate every quarter?

Artur Skowroński and Tomasz Lelek spent a year shipping AI-assisted code across enterprise clients, watching what broke and what survived, then committed the patterns to print in Vibe Engineering: Best practices, mistakes, and tradeoffs, co-published with Manning Publications.

Join Artur and Tomasz for a discussion panel and community mixer, and learn about:
- Where AI earns its place in the delivery cycle, and where it creates new debt 
- Vibe coding beyond the individual contributor: what changes at team and org scale
- Keeping quality and trust intact when half your code has a co-author 
- Staying fast without losing control of what ships

And if you are interested in writing a book yourself, Artur and Tomasz will also talk about:
- Picking the right topic, so that the trend is still relevant when the book is out
- Getting past the writer’s block
- What reputable publishers like Manning are actually looking for right now

After the panel, you are invited over for drinks, networking, and the kind of conversations that don't happen in Slack.

Bonus: We'll be giving away 30 demo copies of Vibe Engineering: Best practices, mistakes, and tradeoffs. Grab one and get it signed by Artur and Tomasz in person.
