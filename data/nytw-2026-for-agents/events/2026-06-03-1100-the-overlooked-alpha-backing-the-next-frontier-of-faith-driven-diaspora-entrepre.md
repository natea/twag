## Description

Institutional capital is built on access to differentiated opportunities—and the most compelling ones are often hiding in plain sight.

Join EMTIC Basileia for a private NY Tech Week gathering designed exclusively for fund of funds, LPs, and institutional allocators seeking exposure to an untapped and structurally overlooked segment: faith-driven diaspora entrepreneurs.

While traditional venture continues to crowd into known ecosystems, a new class of high-conviction founders is emerging at the intersection of global diaspora networks, values-driven leadership, and underserved markets. These founders bring asymmetric insight, built-in distribution, and a bias toward resilient, capital-efficient growth—yet remain significantly underfunded.

EMTIC Basileia is positioning itself as a first-mover in this category through a hybrid accelerator + venture model that systematically sources, develops, and backs these entrepreneurs from pre-seed through Series A.

This event will explore:

- Why diaspora founders represent a structurally mispriced asset class
- How faith-driven leadership translates into performance, discipline, and long-term value creation
- The EMTIC Basileia model as a scalable pipeline for proprietary deal flow
- Opportunities for fund of funds to gain early exposure to a differentiated venture strategy

This is not an impact story—it is an access story. One where cultural insight, conviction, and community-driven distribution create the conditions for both outsized returns and durable global impact.

This event is a part of #NYTechWeek—a week of events hosted by VCs and startups to bring together the tech ecosystem. Learn more at www.tech-week.com.
