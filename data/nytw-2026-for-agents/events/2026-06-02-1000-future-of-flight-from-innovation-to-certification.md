## Description

Future of Flight: From Innovation to Certification

The next generation of aerospace products will not be defined by technology alone. Bringing novel aircraft designs to market requires certification strategy, regulatory alignment, operational integration, infrastructure readiness, public trust, and the capital discipline needed to move from concept to certified product.

Hosted during NY Tech Week at the offices of Polsinelli PC, this panel will explore what it takes for emerging aerospace manufacturers to bring certified aircraft to market and safely integrate advanced aircraft into the National Airspace System. The discussion will focus on certification as the critical path from innovation to commercialization, including the challenges, risks, timelines, and capital requirements associated with developing and certifying new aircraft.

With initiatives such as the eVTOL Integration Pilot Program, the industry is entering an important period where regulators, manufacturers, operators, infrastructure partners, and investors must develop together. This conversation will examine how innovation can be supported while maintaining the safety expectations that define aviation.

Featured speakers will include Nikhil Goel, Chief Commercial Officer at Archer Aviation; Tom Huntley, General Manager at REGENT; and Nolan Giblin of Natilus, with additional aerospace leaders to be announced. The conversation will be moderated by Gerald Clerc and Donald Maloy.

This event is part of #NYTechWeek, a week of events hosted by VCs and startups to bring together the tech ecosystem. Learn more at www.tech-week.com.
