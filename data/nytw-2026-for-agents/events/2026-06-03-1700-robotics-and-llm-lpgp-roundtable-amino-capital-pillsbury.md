## Description

As AI enters its next phase—from copilots to autonomous systems—the convergence of Robotics (Embodied AI) and Large Language Models (LLMs) is reshaping how work gets done in the physical and digital world.

In this new paradigm, capital is not just funding software—it is underwriting end-to-end intelligent systems that integrate perception, reasoning, and action. From industrial automation and service robotics to agentic workflows and real-world deployment, a new category is emerging:
👉 “Service-as-Software” powered by Robotics + LLMs

This private roundtable convenes leading LPs, GPs, family offices, and frontier founders to explore how global capital can align with this shift—and where the most asymmetric opportunities lie.

1️⃣ Capital Flows into Robotics & Embodied AI
Where is smart money moving? From hardware-enabled AI to full-stack robotics platforms, we examine capital allocation strategies across the stack.

2️⃣ LLMs as the Control Plane for the Physical World
How LLMs are evolving from copilots to decision-making engines—powering robotics, automation, and real-world workflows.

3️⃣ LP–GP Alignment in Frontier AI Investing
New expectations on fund construction, timelines, and risk in deep tech and embodied AI.

4️⃣ From Labs to Deployment: Building the Robotics & LLM University Ecosystem
Bridging hardware, developers, and capital—how to accelerate real-world adoption beyond demos.

5️⃣ Long-Term Partnerships & Global Collaboration
Cross-border challenges, trust, and structuring durable partnerships in a geopolitically complex environment.
