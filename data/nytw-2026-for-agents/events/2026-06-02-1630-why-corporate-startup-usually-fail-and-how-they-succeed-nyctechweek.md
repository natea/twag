## Description

This is a curated evening for early-stage AI founders, investors, and corporate innovation leaders exploring the future of AI collaboration.

The evening will feature a fireside chat on what it actually takes to make corporate × startup partnerships work — not the press release version, but the real one. Why do so many collaborations stall after the MOU? What does a startup actually get out of working with a large enterprise — and what should they be trying to get? How do investors evaluate corporate partnerships, and when do they become a liability?

Our speakers bring the full picture — a founder living a corporate partnership in real time, a VC who evaluates them from the outside, a researcher who builds them from inside a global enterprise, and an ecosystem builder who has seen the same patterns play out across factory floors, investment portfolios, and communities on both sides of the Pacific.

🎤 Fireside Chat Speakers

・Jason Shuman — General Partner, Primary Venture Partners. Vertical AI investor and operator-turned-VC with 10+ years building companies before joining the investment side. Lead investor in Multitude Insights and host of Change Order, a podcast on the tactics behind breakout companies.
・Sabrina Sasaki — Investment Partner, Monozukuri Ventures & Co-Founder, Deep Tech Forum. Has worked at the intersection of hardware manufacturing, startup investment, and Japan-North America ecosystem building.
・Akihiko Izu — Co-Founder, Multitude Insights. Building an AI-powered platform for public safety, backed by NEC X and Primary Venture Partners.
・Kay Seo — Senior Director & Head of Strategic Pursuits and Global Partnerships, NEC Laboratories America. 25+ years of global experience in high-tech, with a track record of building cross-sector partnerships across corporations, universities, and public institutions.
・Moderated by Tak Kihara, NEC X
