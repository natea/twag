## Description

Clinical AI is starting to change how care gets delivered. The harder questions are who builds it responsibly, who gets to set the bar, and who patients will actually trust.

Amigo is hosting a small afternoon for healthcare operators, clinicians, founders, and investors who are already doing this work. The panel brings together five people who have made real decisions about patient-facing AI from very different vantage points. 

Komal Bajaj runs quality and safety across NYC Health + Hospitals and has a documented framework for the red flags she sees in AI pilots. 

Kabir Daya is Chief Digital Officer at Thriveworks, one of the country's largest behavioral health platforms, bringing the perspective on an operator deploying AI at scale.

Kathleen Leger is a triple board-certified physician in Internal Medicine, Nephrology, and Critical Care Medicine at TeamHealth, bringing a frontline clinical perspective on where AI actually meets the patient. 

Claire Siegel leads product at Humanaut Health, a longevity and preventive medicine platform building AI-enabled care, bringing the perspective of a builder who makes daily decisions about what AI touches in a patient relationship. 

Jay Shah, Chief of the Medical Staff at Stanford and Chief Medical Officer at Amigo, moderates from the seat of someone who builds and practices.

We are shaping this to be a candid conversation. Expect a room curated for honest disagreement, hot takes from people in the audience as well as on stage, and a working answer to what it takes to deploy patient-facing AI responsibly. When you walk in, we will ask you where you stand on patient-facing AI today. Where the room lands by the end is part of the conversation. Stay for bites and drinks after, including a few we named for the occasion (one of them is The Red Flag).

Part of NYC Tech Week. Learn more at tech-week.com.
