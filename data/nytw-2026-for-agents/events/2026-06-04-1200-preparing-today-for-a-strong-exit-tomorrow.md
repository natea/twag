## Description

Selling a company is not just about the deal. It is about decisions and preparations that occur long before you go to market.

Founders often focus on buyers, purchase price, and closing mechanics. However, the most successful exits are built well in advance. The right metrics, clean financials, strong legal foundations, and thoughtful planning can significantly increase your exit value and leverage when it matters most.

The panel will include an investment banker, a capital markets accountant, an M&A lawyer, and a wealth management advisor. The discussion will cover:
🚀 What buyers really care about today
The metrics, signals, and trends driving valuations in tech and tech-enabled businesses
💰 How to increase your company’s value before a sale
Positioning, preparation, and strategic decisions that move the needle on price and terms
🧠 Building for an exit from day one
Operational, financial, and legal steps that reduce risk and improve attractiveness
📊 Reading the market
How timing, industry trends, and competitive activity shape exit opportunities
⚠️ Common founder mistakes
What often gets overlooked and how it can impact derail a successful sale
🎯 Real-world insights
Lessons from deals, including what worked, what did not, and what founders should do differently
 
You built something great. Now learn how to exit on your own terms.
