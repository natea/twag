## Description

The media hysteria frames a fight between Robot and Human, and the industry is happy to oblige.

​It's easier to have a conversation on "right vs wrong" than to admit the fundamentals are broken: skills, accountability, and the will to hold the industry to a higher standard.

​We don't have a technology problem. We have a discipline problem.

​At this Coffee Salon, join an intimate (max: 12 people) & facilitated discussion around what it takes to move past the bizarre and intrusive to achieve the economic and social outcomes it touts.

​In this session, we will look at:
• Which values we're really affirming when we turn founders into prophets — and what gets erased from the story of every company that actually lasted

• How the discipline that would have kept the focus on real problems and human interaction missed its chance to mandate a seat at the table

• Why the obsession with speed is the industry's own kind of substance problem, and which tradeoffs are being made, intentionally or not.

If you're an experienced practitioner or industry stakeholder who enjoys honest conversation and despises charlatans, please join.

​About Your Moderator: Lindsay Tabas first called out the tragedy of siloed enterprise software systems while getting her Masters at UC-Berkeley in 2006. She was told to earn her stripes before she could speak louder. She has.

​About Your Co-Hosts:

​Evan Sargent spent years as a designer, finding UX precious and slow. She then wondered if she'd just internalized speed culture. She founded Leap Year to help startups build smarter and faster. Together, if possible.

​Lori Sussle Bonanni, founder of elssus, has spent her career helping tech and healthcare companies say what they mean. She has a theory: most technology breakdowns aren't technical. They are communication problems nobody named.

​Pull up a chair.
