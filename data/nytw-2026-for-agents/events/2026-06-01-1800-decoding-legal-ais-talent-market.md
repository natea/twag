## Description

A quiet migration is happening in legal tech. Attorneys are leaving practice to build, sell, and scale the very tools transforming their profession. New kinds of roles are emerging faster than the market can define them - legal engineers, solutions architects, attorney-operators - but comp, role expectations, and career paths are still opaque. This event changes that.

Join current and former attorneys navigating the intersection of law and technology for an evening of structured content and high-impact networking.

📋 AGENDA:

6pm - 7pm —> Mixer w/light bites and drinks

7pm - 7:30pm —> The market right now - where lawyers are landing across legal AI startups, AI-native firms, law firm innovation teams, and more. Plus, a practical breakdown of which legal skills transfer, which ones you need to build, and how to position yourself

7:30pm - 8:15pm —> Panel with 3-4 former attorneys now in senior roles in legal tech share their candid perspectives on making the move—what worked, what didn’t, and what the day to day actually looks like + Q&A

8:15pm - 9pm —> More mixing & mingling!

🎯 WHO THIS EVENT IS FOR: 
- Practicing attorneys thinking seriously about what's next
- Lawyers already working in legal tech who want to compare notes
- Anyone interested, building, or operating in legal tech (and no, you don’t have to be an attorney!)
- JD/MBAs exploring non-traditional paths
- Recruiters (*wink*)
- Investors or advisors close to the space

🚨 You should NOT attend this event if:
- You do not have tolerance for risk
- You’re not interested in AI or how it’s reshaping legal work 
- You think it’s “too late” to make a career pivot
- You think legal tech is just a fad
