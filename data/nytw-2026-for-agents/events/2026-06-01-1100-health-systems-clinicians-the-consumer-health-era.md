Session One | One Health Collective (11-12)

Patients are no longer starting their healthcare journey inside the health system.

From wearables + labs, DTC care delivery, and AI-native care platforms, consumers are increasingly building their own front door to healthcare.

Health systems are responding, but the behavior shift is already underway.

- How do health systems view  the rise of consumer health, wearables, DTC labs, and AI guidance/doctors. 

- How forward-thinking health systems are rebuilding patient access, management, and engagement

- What healthcare organizations can’t afford to get wrong with these shifts underway

Speakers:

Dr. Devin Mann, Director of the Section of Digitally Enabled Care (SoDEC) at NYU Langone Health, leading cross-disciplinary teams across digital health research, operations, and innovation. He also directs FuturePractice, NYU Langone's internal tech innovation lab, and serves as Strategic Director of Digital Health Innovation within Health Informatics.

Daniel Marchalik, MD, MBA, Medical Director of Business Innovation and Executive Director of the MedStar Health Center for Wellbeing, leading venture, commercialization, and IP strategy at MedStar Health, the largest integrated health system in the Mid-Atlantic.

Holden Groves, MD, an anesthesiologist and critical care physician at Columbia University, where his clinical work spans the operating room and ICU. He also leads inpatient AI and mobile solutions at NewYork-Presbyterian, building the tools that bring emerging technology to the bedside across one of the country's largest health systems.

Co-Moderators:

John Joshua, RRT, MHA : Co- Founder, One Health Collective 

Addison Montiel, RN, MBA : Co- Founder, One Health Collective

Session Two | NXGN (12-1)

Patients are arriving with lab panels, wearable data, GLP-1s, peptide protocols, and AI-assisted self-diagnosis already in hand.

What does that mean for the clinicians now treating them?

- Physicians on the front lines of consumer health and self-directed care

- GLP-1s, peptides, optimization culture, and the informed patient

- What medicine looks like when the patient journey starts before the clinic does

Speakers:

David Kessler, MD, MSc is the Vice Chair of Innovation and Professor of Pediatrics in Emergency Medicine at Columbia University, leads strategic cross-disciplinary initiatives focused on designing, testing, and implementing emerging technologies to improve healthcare training and clinical care. His work spans artificial intelligence, simulation, ultrasound, and immersive technologies. He has led multi-center research collaborations, authored over 200 peer-reviewed publications, and co-founded INSPIRE, an international network advancing innovation and scholarship in simulation science.

George Matthew, MD, MBA, FACP is a practicing Internal Medicine physician with over two decades of corporate and startup experience, including GE, Goldman Sachs, Pfizer, Medidata Solutions, Aetna, and served as the Chief Medical Officer of Hewlett Packard, later DXC Technology.  While practicing he also serves as the Chief Medical Officer of Times AI Health, a privacy-based health chatbot startup. 

Peter Rezkalla, MD, known to millions as "Doctor Disney," is a practicing pediatrician specializing in Pediatrics, Public Health, and Mental Health, as well as an award-winning child advocate spearheading the new era of health education for families and global communities. Leveraging his training in both medicine and musical theater, he transforms healthcare through creativity, tech, and story-driven science. With over 200 million views on social media, he is a top-trusted voice among healthcare leaders today.
