## Description

As AI and health technology rapidly evolve, much of today’s infrastructure is still built on incomplete, and often biased, data. Historically, women have been underrepresented in clinical research, product design, and data modeling, resulting in systems that fail to fully reflect their bodies, needs, and experiences.

Join Chief in partnership with Clair Health at Tech Week Boston for a panel conversation on how a new generation of founders, scientists, and leaders are confronting that gap — and what it takes to rebuild technology with women at the center, not an afterthought.

The conversation will sit at the intersection of bias, data, and design, examining how AI, wearables, and health platforms can move beyond reactive care toward a more personalized, continuous, and equitable future for women’s health — when they’re truly made with women in mind.
