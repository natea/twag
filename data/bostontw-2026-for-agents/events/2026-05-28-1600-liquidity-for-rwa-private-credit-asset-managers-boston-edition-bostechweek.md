## Description

This event is a part of #BOSTechWeek—a week of events hosted by VCs and startups to bring together the tech ecosystem. Learn more at www.tech-week.com. 

Private credit is moving on-chain. The question is no longer if, it's how fast and who captures the liquidity.

Join Protofire for an evening of sharp conversation and direct networking at the intersection of private credit and decentralized finance. We're bringing together the two sides that need to be in the same room: asset managers tokenizing private credit, and the DeFi protocols and liquidity providers deploying capital into real-world yield.

What we'll cover:

The panel will cut into the mechanics that actually matter — how tokenized credit instruments access deep, persistent liquidity on-chain; what infrastructure you need to make these assets tradeable, composable, and attractive to institutional capital; and where the bottlenecks sit between origination and on-chain distribution.

This is not a surface-level overview. Expect candid discussion on the engineering, economic design, and protocol architecture that separates functioning RWA markets from static token wrappers collecting dust. Who should attend:

- Private credit fund managers and allocators exploring on-chain distribution

- DeFi protocol teams building lending, trading, or structured product infrastructure

- Liquidity providers and institutional DeFi participants seeking real-world yield exposure

- L1/L2 ecosystem teams looking to attract RWA activity and TVL

About Protofire:

Protofire is a Web3 engineering partner to the world's leading blockchain ecosystems. We architect and deploy the financial infrastructure (staking, DEXs, liquid staking, lending markets) that transforms static tokens into productive, yield-generating assets. Our work is the engine behind TVL growth and capital efficiency for protocols that refuse to be ghost chains.

Seats are limited. If you're building the liquidity layer for real-world assets, or you're the capital looking for it, this is your room.
