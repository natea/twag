## Description

Model capabilities have advanced significantly over the past five months, and the next generation of agents is shifting toward long-running, autonomous systems that orchestrate across multiple tools. This shift introduces a new class of engineering challenges around orchestration, reliability, latency management, and cost at scale.

If you're a researcher, engineer, or technical founder, this is the  event to attend during Boston Tech Week. We're bringing together some of the leading technical minds from the Boston ecosystem for a conversation on what comes next. 

The panel will feature:

- Alexander Amini, the founder and Chief Science Officer of Liquid AI ($291M raised), one of the most notable AI startups in the Boston ecosystem, building novel on-device model architectures
- Ron Haberman, Senior Director, Software Engineering - AI Incubation,  Red Hat bringing the enterprise perspective on scaling AI agents across a large organization.
- Richard Tibbetts, who has experience leading product and engineering efforts at Anthropic, focused on agentic systems and next-generation interfaces.
- A Flybridge member will moderate the panel. Flybridge is a leading Boston VC fund with 22 years of history, backing companies such as MongoDB, Firebase, and Blitzy.ai.

We will also have a fireside chat between Akamai CTO and Flybridge GP Chip Hazard (Investor and Board Member of companies like MongoDB)

The conversation will cover topics such as hybrid architectures that split work between cloud and edge, agent harnesses, and where agentic systems are heading over the next 12 months.
