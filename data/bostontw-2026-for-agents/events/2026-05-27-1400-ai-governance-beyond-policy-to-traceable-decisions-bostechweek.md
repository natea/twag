## Description

AI is already making decisions that impact customers, operations, and financial outcomes, yet most organizations cannot explain how those decisions are made, what context informed them, or who is accountable for them.

This panel brings together leaders at the forefront of AI governance to explore a critical shift: governance is no longer about policy documents. It’s about traceability, accountability, and the ability to defend decisions in real time. As regulatory pressure increases and AI adoption accelerates, organizations that cannot inspect and justify their AI-driven decisions will face real risk.

Join senior leaders, builders, and operators for a candid discussion on what AI governance actually looks like in practice — from enterprise implementation to board-level accountability. If you're responsible for deploying, overseeing, or investing in AI, this conversation will challenge how you think about control, risk, and execution.
