## Description

Tech Week is great, but how many panels on AI regulation can you attend? It’s time to get your hands dirty.

Welcome to the Vibe Debugging Party, an interactive, competitive-ish, "escape room" style event for engineers, SREs, developers, and anybody who enjoys solving puzzles under mild (and friendly!) pressure.

We’ve set up a complex, modern microservices application running on Kubernetes, with a top-tier observability stack - OpenTelemetry, Grafana, Jaeger, etc. pre-configured.

Your challenge: We are going to intentionally, and secretly, break it.

You’ll work in small teams (or solo!) to triage the incident, trace the issue through the logs and traces, perform a Root Cause Analysis (RCA), and propose a fix. No actual coding is required, but you'll need all your observability and debugging intuition.

Is this for me?
If you’ve ever found a bug in a distributed system, understood what a flame graph is, or enjoy the phrase "it’s always DNS," then yes. We will have scenarios for different experience levels.

What to expect:
- Infrastructure-as-a-Gift: A fully-provisioned, broken system for you to explore.
- Chaos Engineering: Unknown, injected failure scenarios.
- RCA Glory: Be the first to figure out "what actually happened."
- Community & Networking: Meet fellow engineers passionate about resilience and observability.
Vibes, Food, and Drinks: Because you can't debug on an empty stomach.

Bring your laptop, your curiosity, and your strongest debugging vibes.

This event is a part of #NYTechWeek—a week of events hosted by VCs and startups to bring together the tech ecosystem. Learn more at www.tech-week.com.
