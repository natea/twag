## Description

Every founder hits a wall — not the funding wall, not the product wall. The one inside your own head.

The constant context-switching. The identity shift from operator to CEO overnight. The decision fatigue that leaves you unable to pick dinner after a 14-hour day of choosing the future of your company. The quiet erosion of your attention that starts to look like burnout—or worse, laziness.

This isn’t a wellness panel. It’s a neuropsychology-backed look at what building actually does to your brain—and what high-performing founders do differently because of it.

Join us for a small, curated founder breakfast exploring the cognitive and psychological demands of building, including attention, decision-making, identity, and cognitive load. Led by a neuropsychology fellow and founder, this session blends science, lived experience, and candid conversation to surface what actually shapes how founders think and operate under pressure.

We’ll start the morning with coffee and light breakfast, followed by a short talk and an intimate discussion with a small group of founders and operators. This is designed to be a thoughtful, high-signal start to your day before heading into the rest of Tech Week.

Expect practical frameworks, research you can use immediately, and a room of people who understand what this actually feels like.
