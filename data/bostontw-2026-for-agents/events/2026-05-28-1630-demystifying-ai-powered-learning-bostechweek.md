## Description

Curious about how AI is actually being used in education right now? Join us at the Harvard Innovation Labs for a relaxed, interactive gathering during Boston Tech Week! 

We’re bringing together students, educators, founders, and investors to explore how AI is shaping learning today—and what it should look like moving forward. 

The evening will feature quick, fast-paced demos from early-stage AI and EdTech startups, followed by a panel conversation on what’s possible—and what’s responsible—when it comes to AI in learning. We’ll close with an open Q&A and time to connect. 

One thing that makes this event different: we’re centering student voices. A high school student will help guide the conversation, and students in the audience will play an active role in the Q&A. 

If you arrive early, you’ll also have the option to join a guided tour of the Harvard Innovation Labs to get a behind-the-scenes look at the space (limited spots, advance sign-up required). 

What to expect: 

⚡ Short, fast-paced demos from AI & EdTech startups 

💬 Panel: AI for Learning – What’s Possible, What’s Responsible?

🎤 Open Q&A with audience participation 

🤝 Time to connect with others in the space 

More on the "AI for Learning – What’s Possible, What’s Responsible?" panel

Moderator: 
💬 Uche B Amaechi, Faculty Chair, Leading Change Foundations, Lecturer at Harvard Graduate School of Education

Panelists:
💬 Ethan Pierce, Founder, Adaptive Reader
💬 Eileen Rudden, Founder, Sankaty Growth Partners
💬 Marta McAlister, Director Gemini for Education, Google

Whether you’re deep in this work or just starting to explore it, this is a great chance to learn, share ideas, and meet people thinking about the future of learning. 

This event is a part of #BostonTechWeek—a week of events hosted by VCs and startups to bring together the tech ecosystem. Learn more at www.tech-week.com.
