## Description

Mellea: Building Predictable, Auditable AI Systems

In this session we'll introduce Mellea, an open-source library that treats large language models, not as conversational interfaces, but as programmable components inside real applications. You'll see how Mellea is used to implement workflows that are predictable, auditable, and reproducible. Combined with smaller open-weight models like IBM's Granite models, developers can do Frontier Model Things but with better robustness and at a tiny fraction of the cost.

Bring a laptop. This session is open to engineers, AI builders, and PMs who are interested in leveraging the latest in open source LLMs and AI tools to build, not demos, but software that scales.
______

This is the last in a series of four "Build-n-Brew" sessions to be held in the afternoon of each day during Boston Tech Week, hosted at the MIT-IBM Computing Research Lab at "Pi on Main" (314 Main Street) in the heart of Kendall Square. Co-sponsored by The Open Accelerator, RedHat, and IBM, each session starts at 4pm and will be a hands-on "build" experience to learn and use the latest in open source AI tech, to be followed by a reception for a little nosh, "brew", and networking.  This last session will be on Mellea.

NOTE: Doors open at 3:30p and space is limited to the first 50 to arrive - If you don't make it in time, we'll leave you with a small consolation prize and an invitation to join us at a future event.

This event is a part of #BOSTechWeek—a week of events hosted by VCs and startups to bring together the tech ecosystem. Learn more at www.tech-week.com.
